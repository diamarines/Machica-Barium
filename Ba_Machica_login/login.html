Understanding Anxiety

The word "anxiety" comes from the Latin word "angor."

The first recorded instances of anxiety can be dated back to the Greco- Roman Era, where physicians and philosophers implemented techniques that are similar to our modern cognitive behavioral therapy. Since then, the terms used to diagnose anxiety have constantly changed, and as well as the techniques used in the medical field to treat it.

The signs that indicate anxiety includes feeling tense, restless, fearful, dreadful, dizzy and lightheaded, and worried. It is usually triggered by stress, conflict, and otherwise undesirable situations or traumatic experiences in life. Anxiety is a normal condition to experience, but persistent and overwhelming anxiety can lead to crippling effects on a person's life.

As of now, there are 5 identified anxiety disorders, such as Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD), Panic Disorder, Social Anxiety Disorder, and Phobia-related disorders.
